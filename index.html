Biography

Bin Chen is an Associate Professor in the Department of Physics and the Center for Solar-Terrestrial Research at the New Jersey Institute of Technology. Prior to NJIT, I worked as an Astrophysicist at the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics. 

He was the recipient of the National Science Foundation CAREER Award in 2017, and the NASA/UCAR Jack Eddy Fellowship in 2013. He is currently serving as a Committee Member of the Solar Physics Division of the American Astronomical Society.  

Research in Dr. Chen’s group focuses on solar flares and coronal mass ejections, the largest explosions in the solar system that can affect our Earth and near-Earth environment, known as Space Weather. We utilize observations from state-of-the-art radio telescopes, including Expanded Owens Valley Solar Array, Karl G. Jansky Very Large Array, and Atacama Large (sub)Millimeter Array, and multiple NASA space telescopes including SDO, RHESSI, Hinode. Research funded by NSF and NASA.
